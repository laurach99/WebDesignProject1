About us

Hribyi Summer University (HSU) is a summer camp aimed to show high school students a slice from the life of a first year university student. For two weeks, you will see how the examination works, you will attend workshops, lectures and you will see what dorm life is like in the town of Hribyi.

Moreover, you get to meet other passionate teenagers with whom you will participate in team building activities, not just summer classes. Of course, the student experience wouldn't be complete without a few parties! 

HSU is a project of Hribyi University Student Union (HUSU) in collaboration with Hribyi University. The main goal of this summer camp is to familiarize teenagers with our university, the faculties and available study programs. 

Get to know our faculties

At Hribyi Summer University you can familiarize yourself with one of our three faculties!

Faculty of Philosophy

The Faculty of Philosophy offers one bachelor program (Philosophy) and two master programs (Bioethics and Metaphysics)

Faculty of Natural Sciences

The Faculty of Natural Sciences offers four bachelor programs (Microbiology, Physics, Chemistry and Geology)
and two master programs (Biochemistry and Geotechnics)

Faculty of Engineering

The Faculty of Engineering offers three bachelor programs (Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Aeronautics and Civil Engineering) and two master programs (Integrated Circuits and Aerospace Engineering)

FAQ

Who can participate?

Any high school student from Mushroomland, from 10th and 11th grade. If you are still a minor, your parent or legal guardian must send us a signed parental agreement after you sent your application.

Is there a participation fee?

Yes, there is a participation fee of 50€. This is going to cover several things for your stay such as food, transportation and the welcome pack.

When does the summer camp take place?

Hribyi Summer University takes place on the first two weeks of July.

What should I bring with me?

Comfortable clothes and shoes, toiletries, bath towel, backpack, raincoat or umbrella, spending money and whatever else you feel you might need.  

Can my parents visit me while I'm at HSU?

Unfortunately, your parents can't visit you once the summer camp starts. But feel free to give them a call every now and then! After all, it's only going to be two weeks away from home.
